About Sokoban

Sokoban is a classic puzzle game that has captivated players for decades. The objective is simple yet challenging: push boxes onto designated target areas within a warehouse. What makes this game truly engaging is that you can only push boxes, never pull them, and you must carefully plan your moves to avoid getting stuck.

Originally created in 1981 by Hiroyuki Imabayashi, Sokoban has become one of the most influential puzzle games of all time. The name "Sokoban" means "warehouse keeper" in Japanese, which perfectly describes your role in the game.

This open-source version brings the classic gameplay to your browser with modern graphics and smooth controls, making it accessible to both newcomers and longtime fans of the genre.

Basic Controls

  • Arrow Keys: Move your character around the warehouse
  • WASD Keys: Alternative movement controls
  • R Key: Restart the current level
  • U Key: Undo your last move

Game Rules

  • Push boxes onto the target squares (usually marked with dots or different colors)
  • You can only push boxes, never pull them
  • Only one box can be pushed at a time
  • Avoid pushing boxes into corners or against walls where they can't be moved
  • Complete all levels by placing all boxes on their targets

Strategy Tips

  • Think before you move - planning ahead is crucial
  • Work backwards from the solution when stuck
  • Keep escape routes open for boxes
  • Use the undo function to experiment with different approaches
